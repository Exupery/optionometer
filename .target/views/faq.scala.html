Optionometer FAQ

  1. Is Optionometer a good place to start for an inexperienced trader?

    The data presented here is most useful to those already familiar with the basics of option trading. If you're a new trader you may want to take a look at our new trader PLACEHOLDER

  2. Does Optionometer screen all US stocks?

    Not yet, only components of the S&P 500, Nasdaq 100, and Dow Jones Industrial Average are screened.

  3. Any plans to include more strategies?

    There are plans to eventually include butterflies and condors.

  4. Why is the data delayed?

    At this phase (early beta) there is not yet the need to incur the costs associated with providing the data in real-time.

  5. Will Optionometer always be free?

    Once all features have been implemented and testing has completed much of the data presently available will continue to be offered without a subscription.

  6. What will be the benefit of a subscription?

    That has not yet been determined, some possibilities are access to more symbols, more strategies, or real-time data.

  7. Is there a target date for completion?

    As of Fall 2013 the plan is to have concluded beta by the end of the year.
